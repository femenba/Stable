# @stableadhd — Instagram Brand Guidelines

## Brand Essence

**stable.** is the app for adults with ADHD who are done with chaos, done with shame, and ready for a system that actually works with their brain — not against it.

The brand does not shout. It does not hustle. It does not perform productivity.
It holds space. It is quiet confidence. It is the stillness before clarity.

**Brand archetype:** The Wise Caretaker — calm, intelligent, deeply compassionate.
**Brand voice:** A trusted friend who happens to understand neuroscience.

## Typography

### Primary Typefaces

**Display / Headlines:** Canela (or Playfair Display as free alternative)
- Weight: Regular (400) — elegance lives in restraint
- Use for: Hero quotes, post headlines, carousel titles
- Character: Editorial, emotional, sophisticated

**Body / UI Text:** Inter
- Weight: Light (300) and Regular (400)
- Use for: Captions, supporting text, stats, labels
- Character: Clean, modern, invisible — lets content breathe

**Accent / Labels:** DM Mono (monospace)
- Weight: Regular (400)
- Use for: Hashtags, metadata, system-style labels, timestamps
- Character: Technical precision without coldness

### Typography Rules

- Headlines: 28–48pt, loose tracking (+10 to +20), center or left aligned
- Body: 14–16pt, generous line-height (1.6–1.8)
- Never use more than 2 typefaces per post
- Never bold a headline — let the typeface do the work
- Quotes always in Canela italic. Always.
- Never use ALL CAPS for emotional content

## Visual Identity Rules

### Spacing & Layout
- Minimum 15% safe zone margin on all sides (Instagram crops edges)
- Content should never feel crowded — ADHD brains need visual breathing room
- Use a consistent 8pt grid system for all layouts
- Whitespace is not empty space — it is the design

### Photography Style
- No stock photography
- No staged "productive person at desk" imagery
- Visual motifs: soft morning light, plants, hands holding a phone, close-ups of objects
- Textures: linen, paper, aged wood, stone
- If using people: candid, not posed — never smiling at camera

### Illustration / Graphics
- Minimal line-based illustrations only
- No mascots, no characters, no cartoon ADHD brains
- Abstract geometric shapes as texture/background only
- No clipart or icon-heavy designs

### Gradient Use
- Only dark-to-darker (Forest Night → Deep Sage)
- Never bright-to-bright
- Maximum 2-stop gradient
- Applied subtly as overlay on textures

### Post Finish
- Every post has a subtle grain overlay (3–5% noise texture) — adds premium film feel
- Corner radius on card elements: 12px
- No harsh drop shadows — use 20% opacity soft shadows at 4px blur only

## Brand Voice & Tone

### The stable. voice is:
- **Calm** — never urgent, never alarming
- **Precise** — says exactly what it means, no filler
- **Warm** — deeply human, never clinical
- **Confident** — not arrogant, never preachy
- **Honest** — acknowledges the hard stuff without wallowing

### It sounds like:
> "Your brain isn't broken. It just works differently. stable. was built for that."

> "Overwhelm isn't weakness. It's data. Let's work with it."

> "You don't need more discipline. You need a better system."

> "Calm isn't the absence of chaos. It's knowing what to do with it."

### It never sounds like:
- "SMASH that follow button!" ❌
- "10 ADHD HACKS that will CHANGE YOUR LIFE" ❌
- "Day 47 of my productivity journey!!!!" ❌
- "You've got this!! 💪💪" ❌

### Caption Structure
Every caption follows this architecture:
1. **Hook** (line 1, visible before "more") — quiet provocation or observation
2. **Body** — 3–5 short paragraphs, each 1–3 lines
3. **Space** — a single blank line before the CTA
4. **CTA** — one action, specific and gentle
5. **Hashtags** — separated, never inline

## Content Pillars

### 1. ADHD Education
Factual, research-backed content that reframes ADHD — not as deficit, but as difference.
Tone: authoritative but accessible. Never condescending.
Format: Carousels, quote posts, infographic-style cards.

### 2. Emotional Validation
The content that makes people stop scrolling and whisper "this is me."
Tone: Deeply personal, honest, no toxic positivity.
Format: Single image with quote, story polls, raw text posts.

### 3. Focus Systems
Practical frameworks that actually work for ADHD brains.
Tone: Practical, calm, no-hustle energy.
Format: Carousels (step-by-step), reels (demonstrations).

### 4. Routines
Not "morning routines of successful CEOs." Real, flexible, forgiving routines.
Tone: Realistic, non-judgmental, iterative.
Format: Carousels, before/after story sets.

### 5. Task Paralysis
Naming the thing that nobody talks about.
Tone: Validating first, then gently practical.
Format: Quote posts, short reels, carousel breakdowns.

### 6. Self-Compassion
Permission to rest. Permission to not optimize. Permission to be human.
Tone: Soft, generous, healing.
Format: Quote posts, slow reels, story cards.

### 7. Calm Productivity
The anti-hustle approach to getting things done.
Tone: Philosophical, grounded, quiet confidence.
Format: Long-form carousels, reels.

### 8. App Features
Product content that doesn't feel like advertising.
Tone: Practical demonstration, outcome-focused.
Format: Screen recording reels, feature carousels, story highlights.

### 9. Build in Public
The human behind stable. — decisions, failures, learnings.
Tone: Transparent, honest, no performance.
Format: Single posts, long captions, story Q&As.
