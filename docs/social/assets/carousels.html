Why you
can't just
start.

The neuroscience of task paralysis in ADHD.

02 / 07

It's not
laziness.

Task paralysis happens when the prefrontal cortex doesn't get enough dopamine signal to initiate.

Without that signal, your brain literally cannot engage the start sequence.

03 / 07

The loop.

01

Task appears in your field of awareness

02

Brain searches for dopamine signal to start

03

Signal absent — task isn't novel, urgent, or rewarding

04

Brain freezes. Anxiety builds. Task feels larger.

04 / 07

Why deadlines
help (and
don't).

Urgency creates an artificial dopamine spike. That's why ADHD brains work best at the last minute.

It works. But it's not sustainable. And it's not living.

05 / 07

What actually
helps.

→ Reduce the task to its smallest possible first step

→ Set a 5-minute timer — not a "session", a window

→ Add a sensory cue: specific music, location, or scent

→ Remove every decision before you start

The focus
protocol.

Do this before every work session.

01 / 05

Clear your
space.

One surface. One task visible. Everything else out of sight.

Your visual field is your mental field.

02 / 05

Write the
one thing.

Not your list. Not your goals.

The one specific thing you will do in the next 25 minutes.

Write it down. Say it out loud.

03 / 05

Set the
container.

25 minutes. Phone face-down. Notifications off.

stable. handles the timer and tracks your streak.

04 / 05

Start before
you're ready.

You will never feel fully ready. That feeling never comes.

Open the doc. Type the first word. Click the first button.

Action creates motivation — not the other way around.

05 / 05

End on
time.

Stop when the timer ends — even if you're in flow.

This trains your brain that focus sessions are safe.

It will trust the process more next time.

Carousel 03 — A morning routine that actually works with ADHD
Pillar: Routines · 7 slides

A morning
routine that
actually works.

3 habits. Any order. Forgiving.

02 / 07

The problem
with most
routines.

They're built for neurotypical brains. They assume consistent sleep, mood, and motivation.

ADHD doesn't do consistent.

Habit 01

Light &
water.

Within 5 minutes of waking: open a blind and drink a glass of water.

Sets your circadian clock. Gives your brain a non-digital first input.

Habit 02

The one
thing.

Leave a note or open stable. tasks the night before.

One task. The one thing that would make tomorrow feel okay if you did it. Morning-you doesn't have to decide.

Habit 03

10-minute
no-agenda
window.

Sit. Drink something warm. Don't optimise this time.

Your ADHD brain needs transition time. This is the buffer that prevents the crash.

The rule.

All three → great.

Two → good.

One → you still showed up.

Miss the day → tomorrow is not the enemy.

stable. asks one
question each morning.

"What's your one thing today?"
