# @stableadhd — Instagram Launch Strategy

## Launch Philosophy

We don't launch with noise. We launch with intention.

The first 9 posts on an Instagram profile are a portfolio. They are the first thing a visitor sees — the grid becomes a single designed object. Every post must serve the whole.

The goal of launch week: **not followers, but resonance**. We want the right 1,000 people to find this account and feel like it was made for them. The algorithm rewards saves and shares more than likes — so every piece of content must earn a save.

## First 3 Carousel Posts (Full Breakdown)

### CAROUSEL 01 — "Why you can't just start" (Task Paralysis Science)

**Slides:**

**Slide 1 (Cover):**
> *"Why you can't just start."*
> The neuroscience of task paralysis in ADHD.
> Forest Night bg, Canela headline.

**Slide 2:**
> **It's not laziness.**
> Task paralysis happens when the prefrontal cortex — the part of your brain responsible for initiating tasks — doesn't get enough dopamine signal.
> Without that signal, your brain literally cannot engage the start sequence.

**Slide 3:**
> **The loop:**
> 1. Task appears
> 2. Brain searches for dopamine signal
> 3. None found (task isn't novel/urgent/interesting)
> 4. Brain freezes
> 5. Anxiety builds
> 6. Task feels larger
> 7. Repeat

**Slide 4:**
> **Why deadlines help (and why they don't):**
> Urgency artificially creates the dopamine spike.
> That's why ADHD brains work best at the last minute.
> But it's not sustainable. And it's not living.

**Slide 5:**
> **What actually helps:**
> → Reduce the task to its smallest possible first step
> → Set a 5-minute timer (not a "session" — a window)
> → Add a sensory cue (specific music, location, scent)
> → Remove every possible decision before starting

**Slide 6:**
> **The stable. approach:**
> Break any task into micro-steps.
> Use focus sessions with built-in start rituals.
> Let the app hold the structure so your brain doesn't have to.

**Slide 7 (CTA):**
> *"Your brain isn't broken.*
> *It just needs a better signal."*
> stable. — try free at stableadhd.com

### CAROUSEL 02 — "The 5-Minute Focus Protocol"

**Slides:**

**Slide 1 (Cover):**
> *"The 5-minute focus protocol."*
> Do this before every work session.

**Slide 2:**
> **Step 01: Clear your space.**
> One surface. One task visible. Everything else out of sight.
> Your visual field is your mental field.

**Slide 3:**
> **Step 02: Write the one thing.**
> Not your list. Not your goals.
> The one specific thing you will do in the next 25 minutes.
> Write it. Say it out loud.

**Slide 4:**
> **Step 03: Set the container.**
> 25 minutes. Phone face-down. Notifications off.
> Use stable. focus session — it handles the timer and tracks your streak.

**Slide 5:**
> **Step 04: Start before you're ready.**
> You will never feel fully ready. That feeling never comes.
> Open the doc. Type the first word. Click the first button.
> Action creates motivation — not the other way around.

**Slide 6:**
> **Step 05: End on time.**
> Stop when the timer ends — even if you're in flow.
> This trains your brain that focus sessions are safe.
> It will trust the process more next time.

**Slide 7 (CTA):**
> Try your first focus session in stable.
> Free. No setup required. Link in bio.

---

### CAROUSEL 03 — "A real morning routine for ADHD"

**Slides:**

**Slide 1 (Cover):**
> *"A morning routine that actually works with ADHD."*
> (3 habits. Any order. Forgiving.)

**Slide 2:**
> **The problem with most morning routines:**
> They're designed for neurotypical brains.
> They assume consistent sleep. Consistent mood. Consistent motivation.
> ADHD doesn't do consistent.

**Slide 3:**
> **Habit 01: Light & water (before screens)**
> Within 5 minutes of waking: open a blind and drink a glass of water.
> That's it. This sets your circadian clock and gives your brain a non-digital first input.

**Slide 4:**
> **Habit 02: Write tomorrow's one thing (the night before)**
> Leave a sticky note or open stable. tasks before bed.
> One task. The one thing that would make tomorrow feel okay if you did it.
> Morning-you doesn't have to decide. Past-you already did.

**Slide 5:**
> **Habit 03: A 10-minute no-agenda window**
> Sit. Drink something hot. Don't optimise this time.
> Your ADHD brain needs transition time between sleep and "on."
> This is not wasted time. It's the buffer that prevents the crash.

**Slide 6:**
> **The rule:**
> Do all three → great.
> Do two → good.
> Do one → you still showed up.
> Miss the day entirely → tomorrow is not the enemy.

**Slide 7 (CTA):**
> stable. has a gentle morning check-in built in.
> It asks one question: "What's your one thing today?"
> That's the whole system. Try it free.
